# MITRE Frameworks Overview

## What I learned

- MITRE is a not-for-profit organization that conducts research across multiple domains, including cybersecurity, artificial intelligence, healthcare, and space systems.
- Its mission is to “solve problems for a safer world.”
- Key MITRE frameworks used in cybersecurity include MITRE ATT&CK, CAR (Cyber Analytics Repository), D3FEND, and MITRE Engage.
- MITRE ATT&CK provides standardized terminology and unique IDs, improving communication and enabling better comparison of security data and incidents.

## Key concepts

### MITRE ATT&CK

A knowledge base of adversary behavior:

- Tactic  
  → The attacker’s goal (the "why")

- Technique  
  → How the attacker achieves the goal

- Procedure  
  → The specific implementation of a technique

---

### CAR (Cyber Analytics Repository)

- A collection of ready-made detection analytics mapped to ATT&CK techniques.

---

### MITRE D3FEND

- A knowledge base of defensive techniques designed to counter attacker behavior.

---

### Other MITRE Projects

- Adversary Emulation Library  
  → Simulates real-world attacks from known threat groups

- Caldera  
  → Automated adversary emulation tool based on ATT&CK

- AADAPT  
  → Focuses on threats targeting digital assets (e.g., blockchain, wallets)

- ATLAS  
  → Focuses on threats targeting AI and machine learning systems

---

## Why it matters in SOC

- MITRE frameworks help analysts understand attacker behavior, improve detection, and strengthen defensive strategies.
