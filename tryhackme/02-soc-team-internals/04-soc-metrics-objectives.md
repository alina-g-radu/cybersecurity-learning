# SOC Metrics and Objectives

## What I learned
- SOC performance and efficiency can be measured using key metrics such as AC, FPR, AER, and TDR.
- These metrics help evaluate workload, detection capability, and overall SOC effectiveness.

## Key concepts
- Alert Count (AC): Total number of alerts received; indicates the overall workload of SOC analysts.
- False Positive Rate (FPR) = False Positives / Total Alerts: Measures the level of noise in alerts.
- Alert Escalation Rate (AER) = Escalated Alerts / Total Alerts: Reflects how often alerts are escalated, often linked to analyst experience and alert quality.
- Threat Detection Rate (TDR) = Detected Threats / Total Threats: Measures how effectively the SOC identifies real threats.
- Service Level Agreement (SLA): Defines expected response times and service standards.
- Mean Time to Detect (MTTD): Average time between the start of an attack and its detection.
- Mean Time to Acknowledge (MTTA): Average time for an analyst to acknowledge an alert.
- Mean Time to Respond (MTTR): Average time to contain or remediate an incident.

## Why it matters in SOC
- Metrics help monitor SOC performance, identify inefficiencies, and improve detection and response processes.

## Tools / activities used
- Analyzed SOC scenarios to identify problematic metrics, suggest improvements, and assign tasks to appropriate roles.

## Personal notes
- Metrics provide insight into both the performance of analysts and the effectiveness of detection systems, helping improve overall SOC operations.
